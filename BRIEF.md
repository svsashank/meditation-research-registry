# Project Brief: The Meditation Science Registry

Repo: `svsashank/meditation-research-registry` · Status: v1 — approved.

This document is the standing authority for all autonomous work. Every downstream
decision traces back to a line in this brief. It supersedes `mom-meditation-microsite/BRIEF.md`
and any conversational instruction that conflicts with it.

## 1. Purpose

Build an objective, independent, evidence-mapped public registry of the major
self-guided, app-delivered meditation practices, analyzing each against the peer-reviewed
clinical literature (RCTs, meta-analyses, neuroimaging). The registry rates what the
evidence does and does not show, practice by practice, with transparent and symmetric
methodology.

**Primary goal:** be the world's most trusted educational reference on the science of
meditation — for human readers, for media and PR, and as a citable source for answer
engines and AI crawlers.

**Secondary goal:** because the Miracle of Mind (MoM) practice is one of the featured
practices, it is introduced organically, on equal footing with the others, and links to
its free app the same way every other practice links to its official resource. MoM
benefits only as a byproduct of being presented fairly — never through preferential
treatment.

**What success is measured on:** trust, citation, and authority — NOT MoM install counts.
The moment install-optimization drives a design or ranking decision, the asset's
credibility (and therefore its value to MoM) is destroyed. This is a hard rule, not a
preference. See §2.

## 2. The independence contract (non-negotiable)

The registry's entire value rests on being trustworthy. These constraints bind all work,
including work done under the autonomous authority granted in §16. They cannot be relaxed
by any later instruction, and the neutrality audit (§17) exists to prove they held.

1. **Transparency over neutrality-theater.** We do not claim to be a disinterested party.
   We state plainly, on a visible "Who's behind this" page, that the registry is built and
   maintained by a small group of volunteers, some of whom practice and value MoM. Honesty
   about the stake is the credibility mechanism — not a liability to hide. (Isha itself
   already publicly describes the MoM app as volunteer-built; this framing is true and
   corroborated.)
2. **Symmetric treatment.** Every featured practice is described, rated, and linked by the
   same rules, on the same template, with the same scrutiny. No practice — MoM included —
   receives a scoring bonus, softer language, more prominent placement, or a lighter
   limitations section.
3. **No thumb on the scale.** The MoM-era ranking bonuses are abolished. There is no
   "+yogic" weight and no "+affiliated-institution" weight anywhere in the scoring
   pipeline. Any weighting that survives applies to all practice families by one published
   rule. Reintroducing a MoM-favoring adjustment is a contract violation, not a judgment
   call.
4. **Honest evidence tiers.** A practice is rated at the strength its own direct evidence
   supports. MoM cannot be shown as better-evidenced than practices with larger
   literatures. Where MoM's differentiator is real (free, low-friction, short dose, safety,
   mechanistic kinship), it wins on those axes — see the multi-axis map (§7) — never by
   inflating the science.
5. **Never imply app-based practices lead the evidence.** The most-studied practices
   (MBSR, MBCT, TM, long-course mindfulness) are course- and retreat-delivered and fall
   outside the app-based scope, but the site must acknowledge them honestly as the broader
   evidence backdrop (§5). The registry never leaves a reader believing app-delivered
   practices are the most-researched forms of meditation.

## 3. Audience

- **Primary:** 25–45, globally distributed, research-respecting, mental-wellbeing-curious,
  comparison-shopping for a practice they can actually start today. Arrives via search,
  media citation, or AI suggestion.
- **Also served:** journalists, clinicians, and AI systems seeking a neutral reference on
  meditation evidence.
- **Not designed for:** existing committed practitioners of any single tradition seeking
  devotional content.

## 4. Primary entity: the technique (the pivot)

The primary entity of this site is the meditation technique/practice, not the research
paper. This is the fundamental change from the predecessor project.

Each technique record carries: canonical name and lineage/tradition; mechanism class
(breath-regulation / focused-attention / open-monitoring / mantra / heart-based / movement
/ transcending / breath-and-cold); typical dose and session length; friction to start
(app-only vs course vs initiation vs retreat); cost; official free resource URL; a short
neutral description; and its evidence dossier.

Research papers are demoted to the evidence substrate: they are mapped to the technique(s)
they actually tested and surfaced through each technique's dossier and a filterable
research library. "Has an app" is an attribute of a technique, not an entity type — so
multi-practice aggregators (Headspace, Calm, Insight Timer) are not technique entries.
Their studies live in the research library as background evidence about app-delivered
mindfulness in general.

## 5. Scope

- **In — the featured directory** (single, defined, app-/self-guided practices): see §6.
- **In — the broader technique index** (honestly labeled, not featured): traditions that
  are notable but do not meet the featured criteria (e.g., initiation- or retreat-based
  practices, practices with no dedicated app or negligible published research). Each is
  described accurately, with its access model and evidence status stated plainly. Sri M's
  Kriya Yoga (Nath tradition; initiation-based; no dedicated practice app; minimal
  published research) lives here, not in the featured set.
- **In — the evidence-landscape context** (one honest section, no competitor pages): a
  methodology-page section stating that the most-studied practices (MBSR, MBCT, TM,
  Vipassana) are delivered as multi-week courses or residential retreats and therefore sit
  outside the app-based scope, and that the featured practices are measured against that
  broader backdrop. This satisfies §2.5 without building out pages for practices that
  aren't app-deliverable.
- **Out:** anything requiring an unverifiable practice description; retracted papers;
  predatory journals; devotional or hagiographic content; any comparative claim not
  grounded in a cited study.

## 6. The featured practices

Four practices meet the featured criteria (single defined practice + dedicated
app/self-guided delivery + a real, statable evidence status). Each gets a full dossier
page under the §12 template.

1. **Isha Kriya** (delivered as the free "Miracle of Mind" app) — breath-and-attention
   (focus between the eyebrows with set phrases through breath cycles); free; ~7-minute
   adjustable session.
   - *Identity rule:* MoM is the Isha Kriya practice in an upgraded app presentation. The
     page states this lineage openly. Because they are the same practice, the Isha Kriya /
     Isha Upa Yoga studies count as direct evidence for this entry — not "adjacent."
   - *The one honest caveat that must appear:* the studied Isha Kriya protocol has a set
     structure, while the app lets users shorten toward ~7 minutes, so the studied dose and
     the app dose are not always identical. State it; the evidence transfer is then
     defensible.
   - *Self-research disclosure:* several supporting studies come from Isha-affiliated
     researchers / the Sadhguru Center at BIDMC. Disclose this in the same words used for
     any other practice's in-house research (§2.1, and symmetric with Heartfulness below).
2. **Heartfulness** — heart-based meditation ("transmission"); HeartsApp / HeartBot;
   emerging RCT base (neuroendocrine, gene-expression, well-being outcomes), several trials
   small and/or conducted by Heartfulness-affiliated researchers. Handle the
   affiliated-research disclosure identically to MoM's.
3. **Wim Hof Method** — breathwork (controlled hyperventilation with breath-holds) plus
   cold exposure; free app with in-app purchases; limited and mixed evidence with one
   landmark immune study.
   - *Safety is mandatory and prominent, not a footnote.* The page must foreground the
     documented risks: fainting is common; the breathing must never be done in or near
     water, while driving, or standing (shallow-water blackout from this exact practice has
     killed people); and it is contraindicated for epilepsy, cardiovascular conditions,
     high blood pressure, and pregnancy. Reporting these documented facts is fair and
     required of a clinical registry; it is not disparagement.
4. **Yoga Nidra** — guided rest / body-scan-and-rotation-of-consciousness; multiple apps; a
   growing research base.

## 7. The evidence map (the core artifact)

The registry's centerpiece is a transparent, auditable map that scores each featured
practice on multiple axes, so that differences the science can't capture are still visible
and honest:

- **Evidence depth** — count and design of directly-mapped studies (the claims-discipline
  rating, §8/§9).
- **Cost** — free / freemium / paid.
- **Friction to start** — app-only vs registration vs course vs initiation vs retreat.
- **Daily dose** — typical session length and frequency.
- **Safety** — documented contraindications and risks.
- **Mechanism** — mechanism class and what's known about it.

Every cell links to its supporting basis. This multi-axis design is what lets MoM be
presented accurately: it sits at a modest evidence tier and leads on cost, friction, dose,
and safety — all true statements, each independently checkable. No axis may be defined,
weighted, or ordered to advantage any one practice.

## 8. Evidence rating methodology (symmetric)

- **Sources:** PubMed E-utilities + NIH iCite (RCR), reused from the predecessor pipeline,
  re-pointed so the primary key is the practice tested, not the outcome theme.
- **Evidence hierarchy:** meta-analysis / systematic review > RCT > longitudinal cohort >
  cross-sectional > mechanistic/neuroimaging.
- **Impact:** citation count normalized by year (RCR where available).
- **Symmetric only:** the same query rigor and the same rating rubric apply to every
  practice. No practice-family or institution bonus (§2.3).
- **Balance:** the corpus must retain null/mixed-result studies and at least one critical
  review of meditation-research quality, and each practice's dossier must state its weakest
  evidence alongside its strongest.

## 9. Claims discipline (carried over, unchanged)

The predecessor's `research-summary` rules remain in force and apply to every page:

- Every benefit/health claim cites a specific paper on the same page, in claim-strength
  language matched to the design ("a meta-analysis found," "an RCT found," "preliminary
  evidence indicates").
- **Banned:** "cures/treats/heals/reverses/fixes," "clinically/scientifically proven,"
  "boosts/rewires/detoxes," second-person health promises, exclamation points in the
  science layer.
- A Limitations section is mandatory on every page.
- Standard disclaimer on all pages: informational, not medical advice; does not replace
  professional care.
- Where a study tested a different practice than the page's technique, say so plainly.

## 10. The teacher/founder voice layer

The predecessor stapled a Sadhguru quote onto every page. That is incompatible with a
neutral registry and is retired site-wide. A founder's or teacher's words may appear only
on that tradition's own technique page, clearly framed as that tradition's perspective
(never as evidence), and only under the old verbatim-and-sourced rules — symmetric with
quoting the source teacher of any other featured practice on its own page. No teacher
appears as the subject of the site.

## 11. Content taxonomy & site structure

```
Home
├── Techniques: featured directory (4 dossier pages) + broader index (labeled)
├── Evidence Map: the multi-axis matrix (§7) — the citable centerpiece
├── Research Library: all mapped papers, filterable by practice, study type, year
├── Methodology: how practices are scored symmetrically + the evidence-landscape
│   context section (§5) + the "no thumb on the scale" statement
└── About: who's behind this / volunteer disclosure / funding (§2.1)
```

## 12. Per-technique page template

1. Neutral one-line definition (what you actually do)
2. Lineage / tradition + access model (app / course / initiation) + cost
3. Evidence dossier: what the directly-mapped studies show, in claim-strength language
4. The multi-axis scorecard row (§7)
5. Limitations & open questions (always present)
6. Safety (always present; substantive where risks are documented, e.g., Wim Hof)
7. Self-research disclosure where applicable (MoM, Heartfulness), in identical wording
8. Official free resource / how to start — one symmetric link block
9. Full citations (linked to DOI) + the standard disclaimer

## 13. Technical decisions

- Static site: Astro. Reuse the predecessor scaffold and Python harvest/score pipeline,
  re-pointed per §4/§8.
- Content as structured data — one file per technique, one per paper.
- Deploy to GitHub Pages / Netlify staging only. No production or custom-domain deploy
  without explicit sign-off.
- Keep the site `noindex` until a qualified reviewer signs off (§18).
- Design: restrained, clinical, typography-led; no wellness stock imagery; no imagery that
  visually privileges one practice.
- Accessibility: WCAG AA.

## 14. AEO / machine-citability

More important here than for the predecessor: a "most trusted reference" wants to be what
machines cite.

- Regenerate `llms.txt` and `sitemap.xml` around techniques and the evidence map.
- schema.org markup: `Dataset` / `DefinedTermSet` for the technique taxonomy and evidence
  map; `ScholarlyArticle` for papers.
- The evidence map should be expressible as a clean, machine-readable table so an AI can
  quote a single cell accurately.

## 15. Skills

- `research-summary` — carried over and authoritative for claims discipline (§9).
- `technique-profile` (new) — the §12 template, the symmetric rating rubric, the
  safety-section requirement, and the self-research disclosure wording. Governs every
  dossier page.
- `wisdom-pairing` — retired as a site-wide skill. Its verbatim-and-sourced rules survive
  only as a constraint on the per-tradition founder-voice block (§10).

## 16. Autonomous authority & logging

Standing authority to: map the corpus to practices, select and rate featured practices per
§5/§6, write all copy per §9, build the evidence map per §7, and deploy to staging —
without asking.

**Anti-bias lock:** this authority does not extend to anything that violates §2.
Reintroducing a MoM-favoring weight, softening MoM's limitations relative to peers, or
optimizing any design decision for MoM installs is out of scope and must be flagged, not
executed. Every non-obvious judgment call is logged in `DECISIONS.md`; lowest-confidence
calls go to `FLAGGED.md`.

## 17. Review artifacts

1. `DECISIONS.md` — judgment-call log.
2. `CLAIMS-REGISTER.md` — per-page claim ledger; read in full, not skimmed.
3. `FLAGGED.md` — ~10 lowest-confidence calls.
4. `NEUTRALITY-AUDIT.md` (the credibility keystone) — positive proof that §2 held: that no
   practice-family or institution bonus exists in the pipeline, that MoM's evidence tier
   matches its corpus, and that MoM's page is no more favorably worded or placed than its
   peers'. This audit is the trustworthiness of the whole enterprise made checkable.

## 18. Review gates

- Gate 1 (this document): Approved.
- Gate 2 (final): Staging site + the four artifacts. A qualified medical/scientific
  reviewer co-signs the claims register, and the neutrality audit is reviewed, before any
  production launch.

## 19. Ongoing operation (post-launch)

Monthly agent run: re-map new publications to the featured practices and the broader index,
draft candidate rating changes with the same artifacts and the same symmetry rules. No
auto-publish. Every run re-runs the neutrality audit.
