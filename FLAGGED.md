# FLAGGED.md — lowest-confidence calls

The calls where the build is least certain, surfaced for the qualified reviewer (BRIEF §16,
§18). These are flagged, not hidden. Build date: 2026-07-14.

---

### F1. Wim Hof death/drowning claims rely partly on non-peer-reviewed sources
The statement that this breathing has caused drownings rests on coroner findings,
litigation (the Metzger case), and journalistic cataloguing, with only the *mechanism*
(hyperventilation → hypoxic blackout) covered by peer-reviewed literature. Confidence in
the mechanism is high; confidence in any specific death count is lower, so the page states
the risk and mechanism without asserting a precise tally. **Reviewer: confirm the safety
framing is fair and adequately sourced.**

### F2. First-position ordering of Isha Kriya could be misread as endorsement
Rows use the brief's fixed order, which places Isha Kriya first. This is arbitrary, not a
ranking, and is documented as such — but a casual reader might infer prominence.
**Reviewer: decide whether to reorder (e.g., alphabetical) to remove even the appearance of
a thumb on the scale.** A one-line change to the `order` fields would do it.

### F3. Founder-voice quotes are not all verifiable to a single canonical source
The quotes are framed as tradition perspective, not evidence (BRIEF §10), but exact
verbatim wording and a stable primary URL could not be confirmed to citation standard for
every practice (notably Wim Hof and the Satyananda Yoga Nidra line). **Reviewer: verify or
replace each quote, or drop the block site-wide** — dropping is acceptable since it carries
no evidential weight.

### F4. Corrupted PubMed DOIs on several Heartfulness records
NLM indexing has stapled wrong DOIs to some records. Verified DOIs are used where found;
others fall back to PMID. **Reviewer/maintainer: re-verify DOIs against publisher records
before any DOI is presented as canonical.**

### F5. RCR / iCite impact metric intentionally not surfaced numerically (deviation from §8)
Per DECISIONS D13, a per-practice normalized-citation number was omitted to avoid a de-facto
composite score. This is a deliberate deviation from the letter of BRIEF §8. **Reviewer:
confirm the omission is acceptable, or specify how impact should appear symmetrically.**

### F6. Isha Kriya evidence tier depends on firewalling SMK / Inner Engineering studies
If the reviewer judges that Shambhavi Mahamudra / Inner Engineering evidence should count
toward Isha Kriya (contra the strict reading of the identity rule), the tier would rise.
The build took the conservative, neutrality-protective reading (SMK is a different, longer,
initiation-based practice). **Reviewer: confirm this firewall is correct.** This is the
highest-stakes single call in the build.

### F7. Heartfulness "friction" rated low-moderate because transmission needs a trainer
Heartfulness is app-accessible, but its defining element (transmission/pranahuti) is
delivered by a certified trainer, so it is not fully self-guided. It was still admitted to
the featured set as "app + trainer-guided." **Reviewer: confirm it meets the "self-guided,
app-delivered" featured criterion, or move it to the broader index.**

### F8. Some sample sizes (`n`) are approximate or completer-counts
For a few studies, enrolled vs. completed vs. analyzed counts differ; the registry lists a
representative figure and describes attrition in prose. **Maintainer: normalize `n`
semantics (enrolled vs analyzed) in a future pass.**

### F9. A handful of DOIs/PMIDs are from very recent (2025–2026) records
Several newest studies are recent enough that metadata may still settle. Links resolve as
of build date. **Maintainer: re-validate on the next monthly run.**

### F10. Yoga Nidra "NSDR" framing touches a popular-science figure
The page notes the secular "NSDR" rebrand associated with a well-known podcaster. This is
factual and sourced, but naming a living public figure warrants care. The mention is
neutral, attributes no quote, and is confined to access/context. **Reviewer: confirm the
framing is neutral and non-promotional.**
